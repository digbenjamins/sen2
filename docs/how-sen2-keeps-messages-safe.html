Alice wants to write a message to Bob. The problem? She has to write it on a giant bulletin board in the middle of town. Everyone walking by — including Eve — can read whatever is on that board. So how can Alice send something only Bob can understand?

Everyone has two keys

In sen2, every person has two special keys that go together — like a matching pair.

🔓 The "Open Key"

You give this one to everybody. It's like a padlock you hand out so others can lock boxes for you.

Computer name: public key.

🔑 The "Secret Key"

You keep this one hidden forever. Only you have it. It's the only thing that opens boxes locked with your padlock.

Computer name: private key.

Alice has both. Bob has both. Even Eve has both! But Alice and Bob only ever share their open keys — never the secret ones.

The Magic Paint Trick

Here's the part that sounds impossible at first: Alice and Bob need to agree on a shared secret color to lock their message with — but they can only talk through the public bulletin board. How?

They both end up with the exact same purple! That purple is their shared secret. And here's the wild part: they never told each other their secret colors. The math just works out so the same purple appears on both sides.

Eve, watching from outside, only ever saw the yellow, the orange, and the teal on the bulletin board. She does not have either secret color, so she cannot make the purple. Mixing paint forward is easy. Un-mixing paint backward is basically impossible.

In real sen2, the "colors" are huge numbers and the "mixing" is some clever math called elliptic curve Diffie-Hellman. But the idea is exactly the paint trick.

Locking the actual message

1

Alice writes her note: "Meet me at the slide at 3pm!"

2

She uses the purple shared secret as the key to a magical lockbox. She drops her note inside and clicks the lid shut.

3

She glues a tamper-sticker on the lid. If anyone tries to peek, the sticker tears and Bob will know.

4

She writes Bob's name on the outside, and posts the lockbox on the giant public bulletin board.

Why only Alice and Bob can read it

Three reasons:

  • Eve doesn't have the purple secret. To make it, she'd need either Alice's secret color or Bob's — and those never left their owners.
  • Eve can't un-mix the paint. She sees Alice's orange and Bob's teal on the board, but pulling Alice's red back out of the orange is so hard that even the world's fastest computers can't do it before the sun burns out.
  • Eve can't fake a message either. The tamper-sticker on the lockbox is also made from the purple secret. If Eve tries to swap the message inside, the sticker won't match and Bob's box will refuse to open.

One important thing to be careful about

🔑 Don't lose your secret key — and don't let anyone copy it!

Every message you've ever sent or received is still sitting on the public bulletin board forever. They're locked, so nobody can read them. But they were locked using your secret key.

If someone steals your secret key, they could go back and open every old message too. So sen2 keeps your secret key inside your computer's special vault (Windows Credential Manager / macOS Keychain) and never sends it anywhere.

The whole story in one sentence

Alice and Bob each have a secret no one else knows. They use clever math to agree on a shared secret without ever telling each other what it is. Then they use that shared secret to lock messages that anyone can see but only they can open.
